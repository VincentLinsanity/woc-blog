ChatGPT Predict Stock Price - 500% Returns from Researcher

WOC Street

(The paper was updated on Sept 2023)


Researchers from the University of Florida have released a research suggesting that the AI model ChatGPT can reliably predict stock market trends. Using public markets data and news from October 2021 to December 2022, their testing found that trading models powered by ChatGPT could generate returns exceeding 500% in this period. This performance stands in stark contrast to the -12% return from buying and holding an S&P 500 ETF during the same timeframe. The study also underscored ChatGPT's superior performance over other language models, including GPT-1, GPT-2, and BERT, as well as traditional sentiment analysis methods.

Implications for the Financial Sector Prominent hedge funds like Two Sigma, DE Shaw, and Renaissance Technologies already integrate sentiment analysis into their trading algorithms. ChatGPT’s remarkable performance in interpreting news could ignite competition among funds eager to leverage Generative AI for an edge.

The Evolution of Language Models

Early Models: GPT-1, GPT-2, and BERT

The first generation of language models, like GPT-1, GPT-2, and BERT, showed promise but lacked the complexity needed for reliable stock prediction. They were decent at understanding text but lacked nuanced interpretation.

Introducing ChatGPT

Fast forward to ChatGPT. This model, powered by GPT-3.5, offers a more nuanced understanding of language, setting the stage for groundbreaking research in predicting stock market trends.

An Overview of the Study

Methodology

Exploring the Methodology The researchers adopted a robust backtesting approach to gauge the effectiveness of trading strategies driven by ChatGPT. They scrutinized a dataset of stock-focused headlines from October 2021 to December 2022, ensuring that none of the articles were in ChatGPT's training set.

Research Team and Dataset

By using web scraping methods, the team compiled 67,586 headlines related to 4,138 different companies. These headlines were then sorted for relevancy; only full articles and press releases were considered while eliminating any headlines specifically about stock gains or losses. Duplicate stories were also removed to maintain the novelty of the information used.

Diving into the Methodology

Web Scraping

The team used web scraping techniques to collect headlines, ensuring none of the data overlapped with ChatGPT’s training set.

Data Filtering

The headlines were filtered to exclude stock-gain or stock-loss headlines and remove duplicates.

Good News, Bad News, and Unknown

The Power of Nuanced Reasoning

The study employed a version of ChatGPT, specifically the one powered by GPT-3.5, to assess whether a headline signaled Good News, Bad News, or was neutral, along with brief justifications. For example, when faced with the headline, "Rimini Street Fined $630,000 in Case Against Oracle," ChatGPT provided an insightful analysis:. ChatGPT excels at discerning whether news is good, bad, or neutral, with detailed reasoning to back its classifications.

Strategies and Their Results

Long-Short Strategy

The Long-Short strategy (buying companies with good news and short-selling those with bad news) yielded more than a 500% return. Impressive, right?

Short-Only and Long-Only Strategies

The Short-only strategy(short-selling companies with bad news) returned nearly 400%, while the Long-only strategy returned roughly 50%.

Failed Strategies

Not all was rosy. Strategies like "All News" hold and Market Value-Weight hold resulted in net losses.

Benchmarking

Comparison with Traditional Methods

ChatGPT outshone traditional sentiment analysis methods, which often misinterpret the nuances in headlines.

Outperforming Other Language Models

Even older siblings like GPT-1 and GPT-2 couldn’t keep up. ChatGPT consistently outperformed them.

Established Firms

Two Sigma, DE Shaw, and Renaissance Technologies should take note. The landscape is shifting.

Boutique Hedge Funds

Smaller hedge funds also stand to gain a lot by adopting AI-driven models like ChatGPT.

After all, who wouldn't want a 500% return on their investment?
